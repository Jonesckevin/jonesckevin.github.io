# Supervisor Checklist — PAR Debrief

> Use this checklist when conducting the **Performance Appraisal Report (PAR) Debrief** at the end of the evaluation cycle.
> This is the formal close-out of the PaCE cycle.
> Use it as a step-by-step script so members at all rank levels get the same clear sequence.

---

## 1) Pre-Debrief Gate Check (PAR Must Be Complete First)

- [ ] PAR roles confirmed with PaCE Manager (Author / RO / SA / IR if required)
- [ ] Author section complete and evidence aligned to FNs
- [ ] If IR assigned: IR review complete
- [ ] RO review complete
- [ ] PARMON processing complete
- [ ] Signing Authority e-sign complete
- [ ] Member is ready to be debriefed (no pending role actions in workflow)

---

## 2) Prepare the Debrief Package (Before Meeting)

- [ ] Provide member PAR package in advance (minimum 24h; 48h preferred):
	- [ ] PAR summary and key sections to be discussed
	- [ ] Relevant FN references used to support ratings
	- [ ] Meeting details (time, location, expected duration)
- [ ] Book private setting (30–60 min)
- [ ] Confirm who will attend (Supervisor, member, additional chain support only if required)
- [ ] Prepare key points in order: strengths, development areas, next-cycle focus
- [ ] Prepare plain-language explanation for:
	- [ ] Role chain (Author / IR / RO / SA)
	- [ ] What acknowledgement means (receipt, not agreement)
	- [ ] Informal Resolution route and timeline

---

## 3) Conduct Debrief (Simple Ordered Script)

### A) Open the Meeting

- [ ] Confirm purpose: PAR debrief and cycle close-out
- [ ] Confirm scope: performance year covered and evidence sources (FNs, duties, outcomes)
- [ ] Confirm expected flow: review -> questions -> acknowledgement -> next steps

### B) Walk Through PAR in Sequence

- [ ] Section 1: Overall PAR summary and performance bracket
- [ ] Section 2: Competency outcomes and score patterns
- [ ] Section 3: Specific evidence examples (FN-linked)
- [ ] Section 4: Development areas and corrective priorities
- [ ] Section 5: Next-cycle expectations (what right looks like early in next cycle)

### C) Confirm Understanding

- [ ] Ask member to restate key takeaways in their own words
- [ ] Address questions in sequence (scores -> evidence -> process -> next steps)
- [ ] Correct misunderstandings immediately and neutrally

### D) Close the Debrief Discussion

- [ ] Summarize 3 points before acknowledgement:
	- [ ] What was assessed
	- [ ] Why the ratings were assigned
	- [ ] What happens next
- [ ] Confirm whether member has any final comments to record

---

## 4) Member Acknowledgement (When to Give What)

- [ ] After discussion is complete, present final PAR for acknowledgement
- [ ] Member acknowledges PAR (author station preferred; or transfer for member station)
- [ ] Confirm member received:
	- [ ] Final PAR view/copy
	- [ ] Summary of follow-up actions and deadlines
	- [ ] Informal Resolution info (if requested or likely)
- [ ] If member declines acknowledgement, document reason and follow CoC procedure

---

## 5) If Member Requests Informal Resolution

- [ ] Explain Informal Resolution route and timeline (mid/end April window)
- [ ] Record exact request details (what is being challenged and why)
- [ ] Document who was informed (CoC / PaCE Manager) and when
- [ ] Confirm immediate next action owner and suspense date

---

## 6) Post-Debrief Logging and Close-Out

- [ ] Log debrief completion date/time
- [ ] Log acknowledgement status (acknowledged / declined / pending)
- [ ] Log major discussion points (strengths, development needs, member comments)
- [ ] Log follow-up tasks, owners, and due dates
- [ ] Confirm next-cycle trigger actions (initial setup and JD/MAP review)

### Where to Log Each Item (Minimum Standard)

- [ ] **PAR system record (Monitor MASS / PaCE):** acknowledgement status and workflow completion
- [ ] **Unit section tracker (local admin tracker):** debrief date/time, attendees, completion state
- [ ] **Supervisor continuity notes:** key discussion points, coaching focus, agreed follow-up actions
- [ ] **CoC / PaCE Manager notification record (if issue raised):** who was notified, date/time, required action

> If your unit has a formal template for debrief tracking, use it. If not, use the minimum standard above.

---

## 7) Debrief Topic Order (Quick Reference)

1. Purpose and scope
2. Overall PAR result
3. Competency and evidence review
4. Development priorities
5. Member questions/comments
6. Acknowledgement step
7. Follow-up actions and timelines

---

## Common Questions to Be Ready For

| Question | Suggested Approach |
|---|---|
| "Why did I receive this score?" | Tie the rating to specific observed behaviours and the rating scale standard |
| "Who can I talk to if I disagree?" | Explain the redress/grievance process; remain neutral |
| "Will this affect my promotion?" | Be honest about the potential impact without over-promising outcomes |
| "Can the PAR be changed?" | Only through the appropriate redress process; explain the timeline |
| "Why did you not tell me about this sooner?" | Acknowledge the concern; reinforce the value of regular FN debriefs going forward |

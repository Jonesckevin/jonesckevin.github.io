# Competency Standards — Sergeant / Petty Officer 2nd Class (Sgt/PO2)

> **2026 Competency Framework Reference**
> Use this document when scoring Behavioural Indicators (BIs) in PaCE for a Sgt/PO2.
> **Score 3 = Effective = the expected standard.** Most members should score 3 on most BIs.
> For next-rank (Effective → WO/PO1 level) indicators, refer to [wo.md](wo.md).

---

## Rating Scale Quick Reference

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ineffective | Does not demonstrate this behaviour. Requires immediate intervention. |
| 2 | Partially Effective | Demonstrates inconsistently. Needs coaching and follow-up. |
| 3 | Effective | Consistently meets the BI as written. The expected standard — not exceptional. |
| 4 | Above Effective | Consistently meets BI and shows depth or initiative clearly beyond minimum. Occasionally shows WO-level behaviours. |
| 5 | Extremely Effective | Routinely demonstrates WO-level behaviours. Rare — reserve for truly outstanding performance. |

## Compact BI Table

> One row per facet for faster scanning and denser reference use.

| Competency | Facet | 1 | 2 | 3 (Standard) | 4 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Communication | Tailored Communication | Communicates without regard for the audience's needs; messages are confusing or irrelevant. | Attempts to tailor communication but inconsistently; audience sometimes left unclear. | Consistently communicates relevant information appropriate to the audience's needs. | Proactively considers what the audience needs to know and structures messages accordingly. | Tailors communication to a broad range of audiences and situations to increase acceptance and gain trust — WO-level. |
| Communication | Confirmation | Does not verify understanding; proceeds on assumptions that lead to errors. | Occasionally checks understanding but relies on superficial confirmations (e.g., "any questions?"). | Uses well-directed questions to confirm the receiver understood the message, as appropriate. | Employs varied techniques to confirm understanding in high-stakes or complex messaging. | Follows up to ensure the audience has understood and acted on the message — WO-level standard. |
| Communication | Open Communication | Creates communication silos; key players are uninformed or excluded. | Communicates with some key players but has gaps in coverage. | Consistently establishes and maintains open communication with all key players in AOR. | Proactively includes stakeholders and ensures two-way communication is ongoing. | Identifies and exchanges essential information with others in a timely manner — Major-level open communication. |
| Teamwork | Collaboration | Distributes work inequitably; does not encourage shared effort. | Occasionally encourages workload sharing but only under pressure. | Consistently encourages team members to share the workload fairly. | Facilitates workload distribution proactively, matching tasks to team strengths. | Works together with others by sharing information and expertise to move forward on goals — WO-level collaboration. |
| Teamwork | Team Morale | Undermines team morale; negative attitude is visible to subordinates and peers. | Does not actively undermine morale but provides no positive effort toward team spirit. | Consistently fosters team spirit and promotes teamwork by personal example and direct action. | Creates visible enthusiasm in the team; morale under their charge is notably positive. | Nurtures team cohesion and morale and promotes the importance of teamwork to team members and others — Capt-level. |
| Credibility and Influence | Inspiration | Does not set an example; actions undermine commitment to unit objectives. | Sets an example in specific situations but inconsistently — not a reliable model. | Consistently encourages involvement and commitment by setting a positive example at all times. | Actively motivates subordinates and links daily work to broader unit objectives. | Motivates and guides others to achieve goals — MCpl-level influence used to lead upward. |
| Credibility and Influence | Art of Influence | Cannot persuade others; relies on authority alone or avoids influence altogether. | Attempts to influence but lacks the credibility or reasoning to be consistently effective. | Consistently uses personal credibility and convincing arguments to achieve objectives. | Influences through well-structured reasoning; builds buy-in before making asks. | Uses credibility to influence others to achieve objectives — Capt-level influence. |
| Credibility and Influence | Impact | Actions have a neutral or negative effect on team functioning. | Occasionally impacts team functioning positively but inconsistently. | Consistently influences team functioning through their actions. | Positive impact on team functioning is recognized by peers and superiors. | Takes actions that have a positive impact on team objectives — Capt-level impact. |
| Commitment to Military Ethos | Military Professionalism | Does not promote or enforce CAF values; allows lapses in professionalism to go unaddressed. | Maintains own professionalism but does not actively promote standards to others. | Consistently promotes and maintains adherence to CAF values and military professionalism in the team. | Proactively addresses lapses in professionalism before they escalate. | Reinforces the importance of professional military standards within the unit — WO-level standard. |
| Commitment to Military Ethos | Duty Before Self | Does not instill this culture; own actions model self-interest over duty. | Mentions duty before self but actions do not reinforce it consistently. | Consistently instills in subordinates the culture and practice of duty before self. | Leads from the front; subordinates model their behaviour after this NCO's example. | Takes actions and makes decisions in keeping with duty before self and demands the same — Capt-level. |
| Commitment to Military Ethos | Military Conduct | Fails to enforce obedience; allows disobedience or defiance to go unchecked. | Enforces obedience in routine situations but hesitates when challenging or unpopular. | Consistently enforces immediate obedience to lawful commands. | Enforces obedience with confidence and correctness; addresses issues early before they escalate. | Maintains a climate of obedience to the code of conduct — WO-level conduct management. |
| Personnel and Resource Management | Personnel / Team Allocation | Sets unrealistic goals or assigns tasks without considering member capabilities. | Sets goals that are generally appropriate but inconsistently matched to capabilities. | Consistently sets attainable goals that balance unit needs with team members' abilities. | Adjusts goals dynamically as capability and unit needs evolve; maximizes team output. | Balances operational requirements with subordinates' personal needs — Capt-level personnel awareness. |
| Personnel and Resource Management | Delegation | Delegates randomly or does not delegate; either micromanages or abandons tasks. | Delegates some tasks but without consistent consideration of priorities or skills. | Consistently distributes tasks considering priorities, resources, and subordinates' abilities. | Delegates with intent to develop subordinates as well as accomplish tasks. | Delegates appropriately, balancing subordinate needs with unit needs — WO-level delegation. |
| Personnel and Resource Management | Monitoring Progress | Does not monitor team progress; quality issues and missed deadlines go undetected. | Monitors sporadically; catches some issues but not proactively. | Consistently monitors progress and quality of teams' work to accomplish unit goals. | Monitors progress and adjusts direction before problems develop. | Monitors and adjusts teams' work making timely and appropriate changes — Major-level management. |
| Planning and Organizing | Planning | Does not plan; reacts to events without foresight, causing team disruption. | Plans basic tasks but does not consistently prioritize or address obstacles. | Consistently prioritizes and plans work to meet team requirements and recommends solutions to obstacles. | Plans comprehensively and anticipates obstacles before they materialize. | Prioritizes and plans work to meet unit requirements, adjusting plans when necessary — WO-level planning. |
| Technical / Professional Proficiency | Technical Efficacy | Has not mastered core occupational skills; significant gaps in technical knowledge or admin. | Has working knowledge but has not mastered key skills; requires support for complex tasks. | Consistently masters occupational technical skills and fulfills administrative responsibilities. | Excels at occupational skills and proactively supports organizational goals beyond AOR. | Excels at occupation and AOR duties, often expanding AOR to assist with organizational goals — MWO-level. |
| Technical / Professional Proficiency | Technical Currency | Does not track changes in occupation; uses outdated practices or knowledge. | Monitors changes when directed but not as a self-driven practice. | Consistently monitors occupational changes to remain technically current. | Maintains currency across multiple areas; shares updates with subordinates and peers. | Demonstrates mastery of technical knowledge including disciplinary and personnel issues — WO-level. |
| Technical / Professional Proficiency | Technical Advisor | Provides inaccurate or absent technical advice; outputs suffer as a result. | Provides advice when asked but quality is inconsistent. | Consistently provides technically sound advice to enhance quality of work outputs. | Sought out for technical advice; improves quality of work across the team. | Provides sound advice to CoC based on technical expertise — Capt-level advisory role. |
| Action Orientation and Initiative | Initiative | Avoids additional tasks; waits for explicit direction on all activities. | Accepts additional tasks when asked but does not volunteer proactively. | Consistently volunteers for or accepts unfamiliar and additional tasks without prompting. | Seeks out additional tasks that align with unit priorities; shows versatility. | Identifies and acts on issues to enhance subordinate and unit objectives — WO-level initiative. |
| Action Orientation and Initiative | Identifying Issues and Opportunities | Ignores or avoids issues; waits for them to escalate or resolve on their own. | Identifies issues but does not act without being explicitly told to. | Consistently identifies issues and takes action instead of waiting. | Identifies issues early and addresses them before they become problems for the CoC. | Seeks opportunities and takes initiative to advance organizational objectives — WO-level. |
| Action Orientation and Initiative | Perseverance | Gives up when objectives become challenging; does not sustain effort over time. | Maintains effort in the short term but loses momentum on extended tasks. | Consistently works hard toward difficult and long-term objectives. | Drives long-term objectives forward despite competing demands or setbacks. | Works hard towards achieving difficult objectives — Capt-level perseverance and determination. |
| Ethical Reasoning | Moral Decisions | Makes decisions without regard for moral or ethical implications; causes harm or embarrassment. | Considers ethics when explicitly prompted but not as a natural part of decision-making. | Consistently considers moral and ethical implications and makes appropriate choices. | Proactively identifies ethical risks in COAs before selecting; models ethical reasoning. | Seeks to understand moral/ethical implications before determining best COA — Capt-level ethical reasoning. |
| Ethical Reasoning | Ethical Risks Identification | Does not identify or tolerates unethical situations; may participate in them. | Identifies unethical situations but delays action or escalates improperly. | Consistently identifies unethical situations and takes timely, appropriate action. | Addresses unethical situations proactively and coaches subordinates to recognize them. | Identifies unethical conduct, takes immediate appropriate action, and forwards concerns — Capt-level. |
| Interpersonal Relations | Interpersonal Adaptiveness | Uses a single interaction style regardless of the individual or context; causes friction. | Adapts style in familiar situations but not with diverse backgrounds or perspectives. | Consistently adapts interaction style across diverse individuals and encourages subordinates to do the same. | Adapts naturally and models inclusiveness; directly coaches subordinates on adapting their style. | Adapts interaction style to respond to a range of individuals with diverse backgrounds — Capt-level. |
| Interpersonal Relations | Trust | Undermines trust with supervisors through inconsistency, dishonesty, or defiance. | Generally trustworthy but inconsistency or gaps create doubt. | Consistently creates and maintains a climate of trust with supervisors through reliable, transparent conduct. | Trust with supervisors is strong and extends to the broader CoC; sought out for honest input. | Fosters two-way trust in dealing with others — Capt-level relational trust. |
| Stress Management and Resiliency | Performance Under Stress | Loses composure under stress and negatively impacts subordinates' ability to function. | Maintains own composure but does not actively support others during stress. | Consistently assists others to remain positive, productive, and composed under stress. | Acts as a stabilizing force during high-stress events; subordinates rely on their composure. | Assists others to remain positive, productive, and composed in stressful situations — Capt-level. |
| Stress Management and Resiliency | Stress Management | Does not maintain a healthy lifestyle; visible stress behaviours impact performance and team. | Makes some effort toward healthy habits but inconsistently. | Consistently creates and maintains a healthy lifestyle; models healthy behaviours. | Actively promotes healthy lifestyle to subordinates and addresses unit stressors proactively. | Creates and promotes a balanced lifestyle including work/life balance — WO-level leadership. |
| Stress Management and Resiliency | Environmental Stress Reduction | Does not identify or communicate stressors; allows them to compound. | Identifies stressors but delays escalating or addressing them. | Consistently identifies workplace stressors, communicates them to CoC, and reduces them when possible. | Addresses stressors before they impact team performance; devises mitigation strategies. | Buffers stress and pressures so subordinates can focus on tasks — Capt-level stress management. |
| Analytical Thinking | Analysis | Acts on incomplete information without analysis; solutions are frequently impractical or counterproductive. | Gathers some information and analyzes basics but misses key factors. | Consistently gathers relevant information, analyzes it, and implements practical solutions; establishes priorities and manages risks. | Produces thorough analysis under time pressure; solutions are well-justified and anticipate second-order effects. | Draws on a variety of sources and establishes priorities comparing solutions and analyzing risks for mission success — Capt-level. |
| Analytical Thinking | Decision-Making | Defers all decisions; provides no recommendations even when asked. | Makes recommendations only with sufficient information; withholds input in ambiguous situations. | Consistently provides COA recommendations even under uncertainty or limited information. | Provides multiple COAs with risk analysis; demonstrates sound judgment under ambiguity. | Makes decisions based on training, experience, and analysis of risks — MCpl-level applied upward. |
| Developing Others | Learning Environment | Does not support subordinate learning; blocks or disregards development requests. | Allows subordinates to pursue learning but does not actively encourage it. | Consistently encourages subordinates to develop their learning plans and pursue opportunities. | Creates conditions where subordinates can learn and reflects on development during feedback sessions. | Provides support for others to achieve their full potential — Capt/WO-level developmental leadership. |
| Developing Others | Performance Feedback | Does not correct errors or avoids difficult feedback; issues persist. | Corrects errors reactively but without explanation or developmental framing. | Consistently corrects errors and explains areas of improvement in a constructive manner. | Delivers feedback with specificity and follow-through; tracks improvement over time. | Provides constructive feedback and recommendations to improve subordinates' performance — Capt-level. |
| Developing Others | Coaching / Mentorship | Does not coach; subordinate leaders receive no guidance on developing their own coaching ability. | Coaches individuals on technical tasks but not on developing as leaders or coaches. | Consistently coaches subordinate leaders to improve their own coaching ability. | Develops coaching capability in subordinates deliberately; tracks growth. | Willingly shares advice and expertise with others — Capt-level mentorship. |
| Developing Self | Seeking Opportunities | Does not seek leadership development; resists growth beyond comfort zone. | Accepts development when offered but does not pursue it independently. | Consistently seeks out opportunities to develop and refine leadership skills. | Seeks challenging assignments and reflects on lessons learned to improve leadership. | Seeks new and challenging positions and tasks to gain additional skills — Capt-level ambition. |
| Developing Self | Receptiveness to Feedback | Resists or dismisses feedback; does not apply it. | Accepts feedback when given but does not proactively seek it. | Consistently seeks feedback and applies it to improve own and team performance. | Uses feedback to make visible changes; demonstrates growth from feedback over the cycle. | Learns from own and others' past successes and mistakes — Capt-level reflective practice. |
| Developing Self | Career Goals | Has no defined career goals; MAP is blank or aspirational without strategy. | Has general goals but no concrete strategy or timeline. | Consistently creates clearly defined career objectives with realistic strategies for achievement. | Career planning is detailed, realistic, and reviewed regularly; MAP reflects honest self-assessment. | Career objectives are well-defined with actionable strategies — standard consistent across all ranks. |

# Competency Standards — Warrant Officer / Petty Officer 1st Class (WO/PO1)

> **2026 Competency Framework Reference**
> Use this document when scoring Behavioural Indicators (BIs) in PaCE for a WO/PO1.
> **Score 3 = Effective = the expected standard.**
> For next-rank indicators, refer to [mwo.md](mwo.md).

---

## Rating Scale Quick Reference

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ineffective | Does not demonstrate this behaviour. Requires immediate intervention. |
| 2 | Partially Effective | Demonstrates inconsistently. Needs coaching and follow-up. |
| 3 | Effective | Consistently meets the BI as written. The expected standard. |
| 4 | Above Effective | Consistently meets BI; occasionally shows MWO-level behaviours. |
| 5 | Extremely Effective | Routinely demonstrates MWO-level behaviours. Reserve for outstanding performance. |

| Personnel and Resource Management | Managing Personnel | Decisions consistently disregard impact on personnel. | Considers personnel impact when obvious but misses less visible effects. | Consistently balances decision-making with minimizing negative impact on personnel. | Proactively mitigates personnel impacts; decisions are visibly considerate and effective. | Balances operational requirements with subordinates' personal needs — Capt/LCol-level standard. |

| Planning and Organizing | Planning | Does not prioritize or plan; unit operates reactively. | Plans for the near term but does not adjust when conditions change. | Consistently prioritizes and plans to meet unit requirements, adjusting when necessary. | Plans are comprehensive and built with contingency; adjustments are smooth and swift. | Prioritizes and plans work to meet organizational requirements — MWO-level planning. |
| Planning and Organizing | Operational Planning | Does not contribute to or engage with strategic planning inputs. | Contributes when asked but lacks understanding of strategic context. | Consistently contributes to the development of strategic guidance and supports strategic planning. | Strategic contributions are substantive and demonstrate understanding beyond own level. | Contributes to the development of strategic guidance and directs strategic planning — LCol-level. |
| Planning and Organizing | Meeting Objectives | Strategies are absent or poorly implemented; tactical goals are regularly missed. | Implements strategies in straightforward situations but struggles with complexity. | Consistently implements strategies to ensure tactical success. | Tactical implementation is highly effective; contingencies are built in. | Implements strategies to achieve operational efficiencies — Major-level objectives. |

| Action Orientation and Initiative | Initiative | Does not identify or act on issues; waits for direction in all circumstances. | Identifies issues but requires prompting to take action. | Consistently identifies and acts on issues to enhance subordinate and unit objectives. | Proactively resolves issues before they reach higher levels; advances unit priorities without being asked. | Invests extra effort proactively into achievement of results — Major-level initiative. |
| Action Orientation and Initiative | Identifying Issues and Opportunities | Does not identify opportunities; reactive and limited to assigned tasks. | Occasionally identifies opportunities but does not consistently act on them. | Consistently seeks and acts on opportunities to advance organizational objectives. | Creates opportunities; brings forward solutions not just problems. | Identifies and acts on issues and problems external to AOR — Major-level outward focus. |

| Ethical Reasoning | Moral Decisions | Makes decisions that compromise ethical standards under pressure. | Makes ethical decisions in straightforward situations but wavers in complex or costly ones. | Consistently makes the right decision when faced with ethical dilemmas. | Ethical decision-making is evident even in high-pressure or career-sensitive situations. | Makes appropriate decisions even when it requires challenging the CoC — Major-level moral courage. |
| Interpersonal Relations | Trust | Trust with the CoC is broken; communication is guarded or adversarial. | Generally trustworthy but inconsistencies create doubt. | Consistently creates and maintains a climate of trust with the CoC. | Trust relationship with CoC is strong; communication is open and honest in both directions. | Creates and maintains a climate of trust and confidence within the team — Major-level. |
| Interpersonal Relations | Interaction Adaptiveness | Uses a single leadership style regardless of context; approach is frequently mismatched. | Adapts leadership style in familiar contexts but not consistently. | Consistently adopts the most effective leadership style for the given context. | Leadership style adaptation is nuanced and deliberate; team performance reflects it. | Adapts interaction style to respond to diverse backgrounds and opinions — Major-level. |
| Stress Management and Resiliency | Performance Under Stress | Loses perspective under stress; subordinates' focus deteriorates. | Maintains own perspective but does not actively support subordinates in stressful situations. | Consistently maintains mission perspective under stress and encourages subordinates to do the same. | Models calm, mission-focused leadership; team continues functioning effectively under stress. | Responds appropriately to others in stressful situations, helping them remain positive — Major-level. |
| Stress Management and Resiliency | Stress Management | Does not promote work/life balance; personal health and team wellness are neglected. | Models balance in own life but does not actively promote it across the team. | Consistently creates and promotes a balanced lifestyle across the unit. | Work/life balance is an active practice; members have visible well-being under their leadership. | Ensures subordinates are aware of coping strategies and a balanced lifestyle — MWO-level. |
| Stress Management and Resiliency | Environmental Stress Reduction | Does not identify or address workplace stressors; allows them to compound. | Addresses stressors reactively; does not anticipate them. | Consistently anticipates and recognizes stressors and takes steps to minimize their impact. | Proactively mitigates stressors; uses communication and resource management to reduce pressure. | Anticipates and minimizes negative effects on subordinates and self — Major-level stress reduction. |
| Analytical Thinking | Applying Critical Thinking | Solutions lack analytical basis; recommendations are not credible or useful. | Demonstrates some reasoning but conclusions are inconsistently sound. | Consistently employs critical thinking and logical reasoning to develop solutions and recommendations. | Reasoning is structured and transparent; recommendations are well-grounded. | Employs critical thinking when formulating plans, priorities, and weighing risks — MWO-level. |

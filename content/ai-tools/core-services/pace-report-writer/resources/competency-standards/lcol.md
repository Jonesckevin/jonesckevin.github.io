# Competency Standards — Lieutenant-Colonel / Commander (LCol/Cdr)

> **2026 Competency Framework Reference**
> Use this document when scoring Behavioural Indicators (BIs) in PaCE for a LCol/Cdr.
> **Score 3 = Effective = the expected standard.**
> For next-rank indicators, refer to [col.md](col.md).

---

## Rating Scale Quick Reference

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ineffective | Does not demonstrate this behaviour. Requires immediate intervention. |
| 2 | Partially Effective | Demonstrates inconsistently. Needs coaching and follow-up. |
| 3 | Effective | Consistently meets the BI as written. The expected standard. |
| 4 | Above Effective | Consistently meets BI; occasionally shows Col-level behaviours. |
| 5 | Extremely Effective | Routinely demonstrates Col-level behaviours. Reserve for outstanding performance. |

| Teamwork | Collaboration | Collaborative efforts are not recognized; teams are demotivated to cooperate. | Acknowledges collaboration when directly observed but not as a consistent practice. | Consistently and publicly acknowledges and values collaborative team and subordinate team efforts. | Recognition of collaboration is deliberate and creates a visible culture of cooperation. | Creates and maintains collaborative relationships across organizations — Col-level. |
| Teamwork | Team Morale | No awareness of influence on morale; behaviour is counterproductive. | Has awareness but does not consistently adjust behaviour to improve morale. | Consistently aware of influence on team morale and adjusts behaviour accordingly. | Deliberate morale management is a visible practice; team morale reflects their leadership. | Is aware of their influence on team morale and adjusts behaviour accordingly — Col-level consistency. |

| Credibility and Influence | Responsibility | Deflects responsibility for unit outcomes; accountability is absent. | Accepts responsibility for own work but not consistently for unit projects/missions. | Consistently assumes responsibility for outcomes of unit projects and missions. | Accountability is visible and unconditional; subordinates observe them owning results. | Is responsible for decisions/programs/processes within or across the organization — Col-level. |
| Credibility and Influence | Art of Influence | Cannot build support for initiatives through credibility or persuasion. | Gains some support but relies on authority rather than reasoning and persuasion. | Consistently uses credibility, reasoning, and persuasion to gain support for unit initiatives. | Support-building through influence is deliberate and effective with diverse stakeholders. | Uses reputation and sound reasoning to gain support for their initiatives — MWO-level parallel. |

| Commitment to Military Ethos | Military Conduct | CoC adherence or code of conduct compliance is inconsistent. | Adheres in most cases but isolated conduct lapses exist. | Consistently adheres to a clearly defined CoC and obeys the code of conduct. | Conduct is exemplary; upheld flawlessly under all conditions. | Fosters an organizational culture of duty before self — Col-level culture building. |
| Commitment to Military Ethos | Courage | Avoids risks or obstacles; courage is not modelled. | Faces risks in routine situations but not in high-stakes or visible ones. | Consistently displays and models courage when facing risks and obstacles. | Moral and physical courage are evident in difficult decisions and situations. | Displays and models courage in convictions and in the face of monumental challenges — BGen-level. |
| Personnel and Resource Management | Allocating Resources | Resource needs for multiple projects are not identified or allocated effectively. | Allocates resources for some projects but not in alignment with higher objectives. | Consistently identifies, allocates, and prioritizes resources for multiple projects, aligned with higher objectives. | Resource allocation is comprehensive and strategic; higher-objective alignment is deliberate. | Forecasts resource requirements to ensure optimal organizational performance — Col-level. |
| Personnel and Resource Management | Resource Inefficiencies | Inefficient resource use persists and goes unchallenged. | Identifies inefficiencies when they become obvious but does not proactively correct them. | Consistently identifies and corrects ineffective or inefficient resource and asset use. | Resource efficiency is a deliberate practice; improvements are measurable. | Develops strategies for subordinates to address resource gaps/issues — Col-level. |
| Planning and Organizing | Operational Planning | Does not contribute to or engage with strategic guidance development. | Contributes to strategic planning when directed; does not independently direct it. | Consistently contributes to the development of strategic guidance and directs strategic planning. | Strategic planning contributions are substantive, adopted, and recognized at higher levels. | Masters strategy, strategy formation, and strategic planning — Col-level. |
| Planning and Organizing | Meeting Objectives | Structures and policies are not in place; objectives are routinely missed. | Maintains some structures but does not consistently align them with all objectives. | Consistently ensures appropriate organizational structures and policies are in place to meet objectives. | Structures and policies are proactively updated; future requirements are anticipated. | Ensures wide-span coordination of activities within and outside the organization — Col-level. |
| Technical / Professional Proficiency | Demonstrating Technical Knowledge | Knowledge is limited to own domain; joint-level breadth is absent. | Demonstrates knowledge in some domains but not consistently at the joint level. | Consistently demonstrates broad knowledge, skills, and abilities across domains at the joint level. | Joint-level technical breadth is recognized; contributes expertise across command structures. | Demonstrates extensive understanding of broader CAF/DND, WoG, and security domains — CWO-level parallel. |
| Technical / Professional Proficiency | Technical Advisor | Does not share technical or professional views with the CoC. | Shares views when asked but does not proactively exchange best practices. | Consistently shares and exchanges technical views, procedures, and best practices with CoC. | Exchange is deliberate and comprehensive; CoC quality improves from their contributions. | Demonstrates expertise in interpreting and applying relevant regulations and directives — LCol-level standard maintained at Col-level. |
| Technical / Professional Proficiency | Policy / Program Application | Cannot interpret or apply regulations and directives with expertise. | Applies regulations but does not demonstrate expert-level interpretation. | Consistently demonstrates expertise in interpreting and applying relevant regulations and directives. | Regulatory expertise is sought; interpretations inform organizational decisions. | Contributes to the development of technical standards, concepts, and procedures — WO-level breadth extended to LCol. |
| Action Orientation and Initiative | Initiative | Does not accept increased responsibilities; leads reactively. | Accepts some additional responsibilities but does not proactively lead. | Consistently accepts increased responsibilities and proactively leads the organization. | Proactive leadership at the organizational level is recognized; results are above expectations. | Takes the initiative to create new opportunities, goals, and strategies — BGen-level. |
| Action Orientation and Initiative | Identifying Issues and Opportunities | Unit-level opportunities and issues are missed or ignored. | Identifies issues but does not consistently act to advance unit goals. | Consistently identifies and acts on unit-level issues and opportunities. | Opportunity action is proactive; unit outcomes are improved as a result. | Identifies and acts on issues or opportunities to enhance CAF/DND goals — BGen-level. |
| Action Orientation and Initiative | Taking Action | Does not anticipate or assess risks before acting. | Identifies risks reactively but does not consistently mitigate them. | Consistently anticipates, identifies, assesses, and accepts risks with mitigation strategies. | Risk management is proactive and comprehensive; mitigation strategies are pre-positioned. | Encourages responsible risk taking to achieve organizational objectives — Col-level. |
| Ethical Reasoning | Promoting Ethical Climate | Organizational practices contain integrity gaps that go unaddressed. | Ensures integrity in own conduct but does not address it at the organizational level. | Consistently ensures integrity in organizational practices. | Integrity is embedded in organizational practices; lapses are rare and addressed immediately. | Displays courage of conviction by maintaining high ethical standards throughout the organization — Col-level. |
| Ethical Reasoning | Moral Decisions | Decisions are not consistently fair, equitable, or morally guided. | Makes fair decisions in clear situations but wavers when moral complexity is high. | Consistently makes fair, equitable, and appropriate decisions guided by moral reasoning. | Moral reasoning is visible and deliberate; decisions reflect considered ethical judgment. | Makes appropriate decisions when faced with ethical dilemmas even when requiring CoC challenge — Major-level maintained. |
| Ethical Reasoning | Ethical Risks Identification | Workplace ethical risks are not assessed; issues emerge without warning. | Identifies risks reactively but does not systematically assess the workplace. | Consistently assesses the workplace for ethical risks and takes timely action with communication/action plans. | Risk assessment is proactive and systematic; action plans are implemented before issues escalate. | Identifies and resolves conflicting interests that have potential to become ethical issues/risks — Col-level. |
| Interpersonal Relations | Developing Relationships | Does not invest in understanding others' needs and challenges. | Understands immediate team members but not consistently across wider stakeholders. | Consistently relates with others to understand who they are and their needs and challenges. | Relational depth is a deliberate practice; understanding of others informs leadership decisions. | Creates and maintains bonds of collegiality and mutual respect with counterparts across OGDs — BGen-level. |
| Interpersonal Relations | Interaction Adaptiveness | Does not consider others' perspectives; communication and decisions reflect a single viewpoint. | Considers perspectives in familiar relationships but not consistently. | Consistently considers others' perspectives, accounting for the asymmetry in perspective-taking. | Perspective-taking is deliberate and empathetic; others feel heard and respected. | Maintains diplomatic, tactful, and understanding demeanour with external partners — BGen-level. |
| Interpersonal Relations | Conflict Resolution | Conflicts occur without warning and escalate; no monitoring or prevention. | Addresses conflicts after they occur but does not monitor climate or anticipate them. | Consistently monitors climate, anticipates conflicts, and acts to prevent or mitigate them. | Conflict prevention is proactive; escalations are rare under their leadership. | Ensures that interpersonal conflict within the CAF/DND is addressed in a timely manner — BGen-level. |
| Stress Management and Resiliency | Stress Management | Stress is not discussed; mental health is a taboo topic. | Encourages dialogue when issues arise but does not create forums for proactive discussion. | Consistently encourages and facilitates dialogue on stress and successful coping strategies. | Stress dialogue is normalized; team culture treats mental health as a leadership responsibility. | Provides support and mechanisms for subordinate leaders to maintain focus — BGen-level. |
| Stress Management and Resiliency | Environmental Stress Reduction | Environmental stress is not monitored; effectiveness suffers without intervention. | Responds to stress when it becomes obvious but does not proactively monitor. | Consistently monitors environmental stress and takes steps to help others maintain effectiveness. | Stress monitoring is systematic; assistance is secured proactively before degradation occurs. | Directs policy changes to facilitate stress level reduction — BGen-level. |
| Stress Management and Resiliency | Stress Education | No understanding of stress effects, coping mechanisms, or CAF resources exists in the team. | Some awareness exists but education on stress and resources is not facilitated. | Consistently facilitates understanding of stress effects, coping mechanisms, and CAF mental health resources. | Mental health awareness is embedded in team culture; members know how to seek support. | Ensures an environment of understanding of stress effects and CAF resources — Col-level. |
| Analytical Thinking | Applying Critical Thinking | Strategies are not weighed; decisions are made without analyzing risk or implications. | Analyzes in current situations but not consistently for future problems. | Consistently analyzes information and weighs strategies considering risks, benefits, and implications. | Analysis quality is high and consistent; strategies reflect thorough examination. | Employs appropriate analytical thought processes to resolve issues and assess risk — Col-level. |
| Analytical Thinking | Analysis | Analysis is based on a single source; key facts are missed in dynamic situations. | Integrates some sources but struggles to extract key facts under dynamic conditions. | Consistently analyzes and integrates multi-source information to create effective solutions in dynamic conditions. | Multi-source integration under pressure is a recognized strength. | Identifies and considers second and third order consequences — Col-level systems analysis. |
| Analytical Thinking | Systems Thinking | Decisions are made in isolation; interconnections between factors and organizations are ignored. | Considers some factors but misses organizational interdependencies. | Consistently identifies and considers all relevant factors, players, and their interconnectedness in decisions. | Systems thinking is a deliberate practice; decisions reflect comprehensive multi-system awareness. | Appraises the complex relationship between competing issues, developing solutions that balance competing demands — Col-level. |
| Developing Others | Learning Environment | Does not empower; development is blocked or ignored. | Provides support when asked but does not create empowering conditions independently. | Consistently provides support to empower others to achieve their full potential. | Empowerment is deliberate; team members demonstrate accelerated growth. | Encourages subordinate leaders to be self-aware and work on deficiencies — Col-level. |
| Developing Others | Performance Feedback | Feedback to subordinate leaders is absent or sporadic. | Provides feedback but not consistently regular or developmental in focus. | Consistently provides regular and timely feedback to subordinate leaders on progress and improvements. | Feedback cadence is established; subordinate leaders demonstrate measurable improvement. | Provides regular feedback to subordinate leaders to foster improved performance — CWO-level. |

| Developing Self | Receptiveness to Feedback | Dismisses feedback or applies it uncritically without discernment. | Receives feedback but does not consistently discriminate between useful and unhelpful input. | Consistently receives feedback with discernment and carefully selects appropriate corrective actions. | Feedback discernment is mature; changes are deliberate and well-considered. | Is aware of own strengths and weaknesses, and works to improve on deficiencies — Col-level. |

| Adaptability | Behavioural Flexibility | Approaches and tactics are rigid; unit objectives are missed when conditions change. | Adapts in familiar situations but not consistently when new approaches are needed. | Consistently adapts approaches and tactics to achieve unit objectives. | Tactical adaptation is swift and effective; team continues performing when conditions shift. | Adapts behaviours, tactics, and approaches to deal with a wide range of national and international organizations — BGen-level. |
| Adaptability | Openness | Resists change; new directions are met with resistance. | Models openness personally but not consistently across the team. | Consistently demonstrates openness when faced with change. | Openness is visible and modelled; team adapts to change more readily. | Adjusts priorities and associated strategies to respond to emerging opportunities and risks — BGen-level. |
| Adaptability | Change Management | Change resistance is not anticipated or addressed; transitions create significant friction. | Addresses resistance reactively but does not anticipate or pre-empt it. | Consistently anticipates negative reactions and implements approaches to address resistance and concerns. | Change management is proactive; transitions are consistently smoother than expected. | Monitors the implementation of change and evaluates its effectiveness and efficiency — Col-level. |

# Competency Standards — Master Warrant Officer / Chief Petty Officer 2nd Class (MWO/CPO2)

> **2026 Competency Framework Reference**
> Use this document when scoring Behavioural Indicators (BIs) in PaCE for a MWO/CPO2.
> **Score 3 = Effective = the expected standard.**
> For next-rank indicators, refer to [cwo.md](cwo.md).

---

## Rating Scale Quick Reference

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ineffective | Does not demonstrate this behaviour. Requires immediate intervention. |
| 2 | Partially Effective | Demonstrates inconsistently. Needs coaching and follow-up. |
| 3 | Effective | Consistently meets the BI as written. The expected standard. |
| 4 | Above Effective | Consistently meets BI; occasionally shows CWO-level behaviours. |
| 5 | Extremely Effective | Routinely demonstrates CWO-level behaviours. Reserve for outstanding performance. |

| Personnel and Resource Management | Managing Resources (Waste Reduction) | Wasteful practices persist and go unaddressed. | Identifies waste when it is obvious but does not systematically seek and correct it. | Consistently identifies and corrects wasteful practices to optimize resources. | Resource optimization is a visible practice; efficiency improvements are measurable. | Monitors, prioritizes, and relocates conflicting resources to ensure objectives are achieved — CWO-level. |
| Personnel and Resource Management | Monitoring Progress | Activities and programs proceed without monitoring; issues surface only after failure. | Monitors some programs but has gaps in oversight of activities and processes. | Consistently ensures that activities, programs, and processes are monitored. | Monitoring includes quality assessment; course corrections are frequent and effective. | Oversees a range of significant programs/projects and time-sensitive issues using appropriate internal and external resources — CWO-level. |
| Planning and Organizing | Planning | Does not plan at the organizational level; reacts to requirements rather than anticipating them. | Plans for the near term but lacks contingency thinking for organizational-level obstacles. | Consistently prioritizes and plans to meet organizational requirements with contingency plans in place. | Organizational planning is comprehensive; contingencies are prepared before obstacles arise. | Prioritizes and plans work to meet the CAF/DND's requirements with contingency plans in place — CWO-level. |
| Planning and Organizing | Meeting Objectives | Activities and personnel are not coordinated; unit objectives are frequently missed. | Coordinates in routine situations but not effectively under competing demands. | Consistently coordinates activities and personnel to ensure unit objectives are achieved. | Coordination is seamless; multiple objectives are balanced with minimal waste or friction. | Ensures structures and support are aligned to meet organizational objectives — CWO-level. |
| Technical / Professional Proficiency | Technical Efficacy | Significant gaps in occupational or AOR performance; cannot assist beyond core duties. | Meets AOR duties but does not consistently exceed or expand beyond them. | Consistently excels at occupation and AOR duties and expands to assist organizational goals. | AOR mastery is recognized; contributions to organizational goals are tangible and sought. | Demonstrates and shares mastery of organizational knowledge including joint-level domains — LCol-level. |
| Technical / Professional Proficiency | Demonstrating Technical Knowledge | Technical knowledge is limited to own occupation; disciplinary and personnel issues are not mastered. | Has some cross-occupational knowledge but cannot demonstrate mastery of disciplinary/personnel issues. | Consistently demonstrates knowledge beyond own occupation with mastery of disciplinary and personnel issues. | Technical expertise is multi-domain; sought as a knowledge resource across the organization. | Demonstrates broad knowledge, skills, and abilities in a variety of domains and contexts — LCol-level. |

| Action Orientation and Initiative | Perseverance | Abandons challenging objectives when conditions worsen or additional demands arise. | Perseveres in the short term but consistency fades under sustained pressure. | Consistently perseveres toward challenging organizational objectives despite adverse conditions. | Persistence under pressure is a recognized strength; team takes direction from their example. | Focuses on longer range CAF/DND objectives despite daily obstacles and unforeseen events — CWO-level. |
| Action Orientation and Initiative | Taking Action | Does not shift priorities; frozen or slow when opportunities or risks emerge. | Reacts to emerging situations but does not consistently align actions with Commander's Intent. | Consistently shifts priorities to capitalize on opportunities and manage risks in support of CI. | Priority shifting is decisive and well-communicated; team adapts rapidly to emerging conditions. | Anticipates, identifies, assesses, and accepts risks with mitigation strategies in place — LCol-level. |
| Ethical Reasoning | Promoting Ethical Climate | Ethical expectations are unclear; fear of reprisal inhibits reporting. | Ethical expectations are communicated but a reprisal-free environment is not consistently maintained. | Consistently promotes clear ethical expectations and a reprisal-free environment across the unit. | Ethical climate is visibly strong; integrity is embedded in unit practices, programs, and policies. | Fosters an ethical climate by holding self and subordinate leaders to high ethical standards — CWO-level. |
| Ethical Reasoning | Moral Decisions | Avoids or defers decisions when faced with moral conflict. | Makes ethical decisions in clear-cut situations but struggles with morally ambiguous options. | Consistently demonstrates moral courage in choosing the best option when options conflict. | Moral decision-making is deliberate and visible; subordinates model their approach. | Stewards the Profession of Arms by promoting ethical decision-making practices — CWO-level. |
| Interpersonal Relations | Trust | Trust within the CAF/DND network is not established under their leadership. | Trust exists within own team but is not extended to CAF/DND-level relationships. | Consistently creates and maintains a climate of trust within CAF/DND. | Organizational trust is deliberate and maintained through consistent behaviour and transparency. | Assists subordinate leaders with creating and maintaining a climate of trust within CAF/DND — CWO-level. |
| Interpersonal Relations | Culture of Respect | Does not model respect; disrespectful conduct is present or tolerated. | Models respect personally but does not create a visible climate of respect. | Consistently models and promotes a climate of respect throughout their sphere of influence. | Respect is a defining feature of their leadership climate; members reflect it in behaviour. | Leads by fostering an environment of genuine concern for members and their families — Col-level. |
| Interpersonal Relations | Interaction Adaptiveness | Does not adapt to individual sensitivities; interactions are tone-deaf to background and culture. | Adapts to obvious cultural differences but misses subtler sensitivities. | Consistently adapts to individual sensitivities, backgrounds, and cultures within the CAF/DND. | Cultural adaptiveness is a recognized strength; diverse relationships are built with ease. | Adapts appropriately to sensitivities, backgrounds, and cultures within and outside the CAF/DND — CWO-level. |
| Stress Management and Resiliency | Stress Management | Subordinates are unaware of coping strategies; stress-related problems go unaddressed. | Shares coping strategies when issues arise but does not build ongoing awareness. | Consistently ensures subordinates are aware of coping strategies and a balanced lifestyle. | Coping strategy awareness is embedded in team culture; members proactively self-manage. | Personifies and models active and effective coping strategies and a balanced lifestyle — CWO-level. |

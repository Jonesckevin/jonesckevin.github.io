# Competency Standards — Master Corporal / Leading Seaman (MCpl/LS)

> **2026 Competency Framework Reference**
> Use this document when scoring Behavioural Indicators (BIs) in PaCE for a MCpl/LS.
> **Score 3 = Effective = the expected standard.**
> For next-rank indicators, refer to [sgt.md](sgt.md).

---

## Rating Scale Quick Reference

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ineffective | Does not demonstrate this behaviour. Requires immediate intervention. |
| 2 | Partially Effective | Demonstrates inconsistently. Needs coaching and follow-up. |
| 3 | Effective | Consistently meets the BI as written. The expected standard. |
| 4 | Above Effective | Consistently meets BI; occasionally shows Sgt-level behaviours. |
| 5 | Extremely Effective | Routinely demonstrates Sgt-level behaviours. Reserve for outstanding performance. |

## Compact BI Table

> One row per facet for faster scanning and denser reference use.

| Competency | Facet | 1 | 2 | 3 (Standard) | 4 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Communication | Tailored Communication | Uses a single communication style regardless of audience; causes confusion. | Makes minor adjustments to communication but does not meaningfully adapt to the audience. | Consistently adapts communication style to facilitate understanding across different audiences. | Adapts communication with skill and empathy; feedback confirms messages are received clearly. | Communicates relevant information to meet the needs of the audience — Sgt-level tailored communication. |
| Communication | Confirmation | Does not confirm understanding; acts on misunderstood instructions. | Occasionally confirms understanding but not consistently or with sufficient depth. | Consistently uses active listening and questioning to confirm own understanding. | Applies active listening techniques fluently; rarely misunderstands directions. | Confirms the receiver understood the message using well-directed questions — Sgt-level confirmation. |
| Communication | Open Communication | Withholds information or updates; creates information gaps that affect unit performance. | Informs some stakeholders but inconsistently; gaps in communication cause problems. | Consistently keeps superiors, peers, and subordinates informed on progress and goals. | Proactively communicates; anticipates what others need to know before being asked. | Establishes open lines of communication with key players in AOR — Sgt-level open communication. |
| Teamwork | Team Optimization | Does not support new member integration; new arrivals feel isolated or uninformed. | Assists new members when asked but does not proactively integrate them. | Consistently and quickly integrates new team members into the team. | Integrates new members with a deliberate plan; their performance ramp-up time is notably short. | Promotes team cohesion through member integration at a level expected of a Sgt. |
| Teamwork | Team Morale | Damages team cohesion through divisive behaviour or negative attitude. | Does not undermine cohesion but also does not contribute to building it. | Consistently promotes team cohesion through actions and interactions. | Team cohesion under their influence is visibly stronger; morale is higher. | Fosters team spirit and promotes the importance of teamwork — Sgt-level morale leadership. |
| Teamwork | Team Communication | Hoards information or discourages others from sharing ideas. | Shares own information but does not encourage others to do the same. | Consistently encourages sharing and sets the example by communicating frequently. | Creates a visible culture of open communication within their section. | Encourages all team members to share ideas and communicate to achieve objectives — Capt-level. |
| Credibility and Influence | Confidence | Subordinates and peers lack confidence in their reliability or judgment. | Inspires confidence in some situations but uncertainty exists in others. | Consistently inspires confidence of subordinates and peers through demonstrated competence and reliability. | Confidence in their leadership is strong enough that it extends beyond their immediate team. | Inspires the confidence of superiors as well — Capt-level credibility. |
| Credibility and Influence | Inspiration | Does not motivate others; subordinates work minimally or without direction. | Motivates in specific situations but not consistently across the team. | Consistently motivates and guides others toward goal achievement. | Inspiration is proactive; creates personal commitment in subordinates to unit objectives. | Encourages involvement and commitment by constantly setting an example — Sgt-level inspiration. |
| Credibility and Influence | Art of Influence | Cannot build support for initiatives; meets resistance without strategy. | Gains some support but relies on authority rather than persuasion. | Consistently convinces subordinates and peers to support their initiatives through credible reasoning. | Builds coalitions and support through genuine persuasion; influence is durable. | Uses credibility and convincing arguments to achieve objectives — Sgt-level influence. |
| Commitment to Military Ethos | Military Professionalism | Conduct on or off duty is unprofessional; sets a negative example. | Demonstrates professionalism on duty but lapses when not in view of CoC. | Consistently demonstrates professionalism on and off duty, setting a positive example. | Professionalism is notable and recognized; peers look to them as a standard-bearer. | Promotes and maintains adherence to CAF values and military professionalism — Sgt-level standard. |
| Commitment to Military Ethos | Duty Before Self | Consistently prioritizes personal interest over duty. | Generally compliant with duty before self but self-interest emerges under pressure. | Consistently takes actions and decisions in keeping with duty before self. | Demonstrates duty before self even in personally costly situations without complaint. | Instills in subordinates the culture of duty before self — Sgt-level cultural leadership. |
| Commitment to Military Ethos | Military Conduct | Undermines or circumvents the CoC; code of conduct violations occur. | Adheres to CoC in most cases but requires reminders or has had isolated conduct issues. | Consistently adheres to the CoC and obeys the code of conduct. | Adheres flawlessly and reinforces the code of conduct to those around them. | Enforces immediate obedience to lawful commands — Sgt-level conduct standard. |
| Personnel and Resource Management | Personnel / Team Allocation | Assigns tasks without regard for individual capabilities or team dynamics. | Has some awareness of subordinate capabilities but does not consistently apply this to team development. | Consistently uses knowledge of subordinates to build and develop effective teams. | Proactively gathers knowledge of subordinates and applies it strategically to optimize team composition. | Sets attainable goals that balance unit needs with member capabilities — Sgt-level planning. |
| Personnel and Resource Management | Managing Personnel | Does not identify impediments; subordinates are left without direction when obstacles arise. | Identifies impediments when obvious but does not proactively set COAs. | Consistently identifies impediments and sets priorities and COAs for subordinates. | Addresses impediments before they impact timelines; maintains subordinate focus on goals. | Distributes tasks considering priorities, resources, and skills — Sgt-level delegation standard. |
| Personnel and Resource Management | Monitoring Progress | Does not monitor subordinates' work; quality and deadline issues go undetected. | Monitors some aspects of subordinate work but misses gaps. | Consistently monitors progress and quality; makes timely and appropriate adjustments. | Monitoring is proactive; catches issues early and coaches before errors are made. | Monitors progress and quality of teams' work to accomplish unit goals — Sgt-level standard. |
| Planning and Organizing | Planning | Does not plan; subordinates lack direction and priorities are unclear. | Plans basic tasks but does not consistently prioritize or address obstacles. | Consistently sets priorities, develops plans, and identifies and addresses obstacles. | Plans comprehensively with contingency for foreseeable obstacles. | Prioritizes and plans work to meet team requirements — Sgt-level planning. |
| Technical / Professional Proficiency | Technical Efficacy | Cannot perform key tasks in AOR or occupation; significant capability gaps. | Performs some tasks but has gaps in AOR or occupational knowledge. | Consistently performs tasks specific to their AOR and occupation. | Performs all tasks proficiently and begins to assist with tasks beyond their AOR. | Masters occupational technical skills and administrative responsibilities — Sgt-level proficiency. |
| Technical / Professional Proficiency | Demonstrating Technical Knowledge | Does not refine technical knowledge; applies only base-level training. | Applies technical knowledge but does not actively refine or build upon it. | Consistently refines and applies relevant knowledge, skills, abilities, and qualifications. | Demonstrates mastery across most occupational knowledge areas. | Demonstrates knowledge and skills at the tactical level — Capt-level knowledge standard. |
| Technical / Professional Proficiency | Policy / Program Application | Does not enforce regulations; allows non-compliance to persist. | Enforces regulations when directly involved but does not actively monitor compliance. | Consistently enforces relevant regulations, orders, policies, and directives. | Enforces policy proactively and uses it to guide team conduct. | Monitors changes in own occupation to maintain technical currency — Sgt-level standard. |
| Action Orientation and Initiative | Initiative | Does only what is explicitly assigned; avoids additional tasks. | Accepts additional tasks when asked but does not independently seek them out. | Consistently undertakes tasks beyond routine demands without being prompted. | Proactively identifies and addresses unit needs before being directed. | Takes initiative by volunteering for unfamiliar or additional tasks — Sgt-level initiative. |
| Action Orientation and Initiative | Identifying Issues and Opportunities | Does not identify or report problem areas; issues surprise the CoC. | Identifies issues reactively but delays advising CoC. | Consistently recognizes problem areas and advises CoC in a timely manner. | Identifies emerging issues early and advises CoC with context and options. | Identifies and acts on issues instead of waiting for direction — Sgt-level initiative. |
| Action Orientation and Initiative | Taking Action | Acts without informing CoC; creates risk without awareness at higher levels. | Informs CoC in routine situations but misses risk communication in complex ones. | Consistently keeps CoC informed of risks associated with courses of action. | Provides proactive risk analysis alongside action recommendations. | Takes appropriate and timely action — Cpl-level mastered, with MCpl-level risk awareness. |
| Ethical Reasoning | Promoting Ethical Climate | Does not promote ethics; may model or tolerate dishonest behaviour. | Reminds subordinates of ethical standards when issues arise but not proactively. | Consistently promotes transparent and honest behaviours and ensures integrity in subordinates. | Creates a visible ethical climate; subordinates understand expectations without needing reminders. | Shows transparency and consistency in how they manage and make decisions — Sgt-level ethical climate. |
| Ethical Reasoning | Ethical Risks Identification | Does not identify or ignores unethical conduct. | Identifies unethical conduct but delays acting or does not escalate. | Consistently identifies unethical conduct, takes appropriate action, and forwards to superiors. | Addresses ethical risks proactively before they become incidents. | Identifies unethical situations and takes timely action to address them — Sgt-level ethical response. |
| Interpersonal Relations | Developing Relationships | Does not encourage relationship building; team is fragmented. | Builds own relationships but does not actively foster them among subordinates. | Consistently encourages relationship building among subordinates. | Deliberately creates opportunities for subordinates to build rapport and trust. | Develops and maintains interpersonal relationships that contribute to mission success — Capt-level. |
| Interpersonal Relations | Trust | Trust with subordinates is absent or broken; creates a climate of suspicion. | Trust exists but is largely one-directional; subordinates do not fully trust them. | Consistently fosters two-way trust in dealings with subordinates. | Trust is strong and visible; subordinates speak openly and honestly with them. | Creates and maintains a climate of trust with supervisors — Sgt-level trust. |
| Interpersonal Relations | Concern for Others | Shows no concern for team members' well-being; personal issues go unnoticed. | Shows concern when members raise issues but does not proactively check in. | Consistently demonstrates genuine concern for team members and their families. | Proactively monitors welfare and connects members with available support. | Represents the concerns of subordinates to superiors where appropriate — Sgt-level advocacy. |
| Stress Management and Resiliency | Performance Under Stress | Loses composure under sustained stress; negatively impacts team. | Maintains composure in isolated stress but degrades under sustained pressure. | Consistently conveys positive outlook and manages multiple objectives during periods of sustained stress. | Visibly stabilizes the team during stressful periods; maintains perspective under pressure. | Assists others to remain positive, productive, and composed in stressful situations — Sgt-level resiliency. |
| Stress Management and Resiliency | Stress Management | Does not apply coping strategies; stress degrades performance and relationships. | Uses some coping strategies but not consistently; occasional stress-related issues arise. | Consistently applies effective coping strategies to prevent stress-related problems. | Applies coping strategies proactively and supports team members in doing the same. | Creates and maintains a healthy lifestyle — Sgt-level self-management. |
| Analytical Thinking | Applying Critical Thinking Skills | Does not apply critical thinking; decisions are impulsive or illogical. | Demonstrates some logical reasoning but gaps in analysis cause preventable errors. | Consistently demonstrates critical thinking and logical reasoning in tasks and decisions. | Applies structured reasoning to complex problems; conclusions are well-reasoned. | Employs critical thinking skills and logical reasoning in developing COAs and solutions — Capt-level. |
| Analytical Thinking | Analysis | Does not analyze before acting; does not compare alternatives. | Compares some alternatives but without rigorous risk assessment. | Consistently establishes priorities, compares alternatives, and analyzes risks. | Analytical process is comprehensive; rarely overlooks key factors. | Gathers and analyzes relevant information before developing practical solutions — Sgt-level analysis. |
| Analytical Thinking | Decision-Making | Makes decisions impulsively or defers all decisions; not calibrated to training or analysis. | Applies training and experience inconsistently in decision-making. | Consistently makes decisions grounded in training, experience, and risk analysis. | Decisions are notably sound; demonstrates mature judgment beyond the rank. | Makes recommendations for COAs even with limited information — Sgt-level decisiveness. |
| Developing Others | Learning Environment | Does not support others' development; blocking or indifferent. | Provides support when asked but does not create an environment that fosters growth. | Consistently provides support for others to achieve their full potential. | Actively creates conditions for subordinates to grow and succeed. | Encourages subordinates to be actively involved in developing their learning plans — Sgt-level. |
| Developing Others | Performance Feedback | Gives no feedback or provides only negative criticism without developmental context. | Provides feedback but based on subjective impressions rather than objective criteria. | Consistently provides constructive feedback to subordinates using objective criteria. | Feedback is specific, timely, and tied to observable behaviours; results in clear improvement. | Corrects subordinates' errors and explains areas of improvement — Sgt-level feedback quality. |
| Developing Others | Coaching / Mentorship | Does not coach; subordinates receive no developmental guidance. | Coaches on technical performance but not on broader development. | Consistently coaches subordinates on strengths and weaknesses for developmental purposes. | Coaching is deliberate and tied to subordinate career goals; tracks progress over time. | Coaches subordinate leaders so they can sharpen their own coaching skills — Sgt-level. |
| Developing Self | Seeking Opportunities | Does not seek developmental opportunities; relies entirely on what is mandated. | Accepts development when provided but does not independently seek variety. | Consistently seeks a variety of developmental opportunities to meet own needs and hone leadership. | Pursues challenging cross-functional or leadership opportunities beyond their role. | Seeks new and challenging positions and tasks to gain additional skills — Capt-level ambition. |
| Developing Self | Career Goals | Has no defined career goals or strategy. | Has general goals but no actionable strategy. | Consistently creates clearly defined career objectives with realistic strategies. | Career planning is detailed, reviewed regularly, and supported by honest self-assessment. | Career objectives are clearly defined with actionable strategies — standard consistent across ranks. |
